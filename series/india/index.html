Series Overview

What This Series Covers

China's rise has fundamentally altered the geopolitical landscape of Asia. Its Belt and Road Initiative, military expansion in the South China Sea, and economic leverage over smaller nations represent a new form of regional hegemony.

This series examines India's position as a credible democratic counterbalance — its alliances, economic diplomacy, historical ties with neighbouring nations, and the strategic choices it faces in the decade ahead.

We approach this critically and without cheerleading for any state. Our analysis is evidence-based and focused on what genuine multipolarity and democratic cooperation require in practice.

Key Questions This Series Addresses

  • What are China's strategic objectives in South and Southeast Asia?
  • How does India's democratic character shape its foreign policy options?
  • What is the role of QUAD, ASEAN, and other multilateral frameworks?
  • How are smaller nations navigating between Beijing and New Delhi?
  • What economic alternatives can India offer to BRI-dependent states?
  • What does a stable, multipolar Asia actually look like?
